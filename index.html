Measurement Disclaimer

All dimensions shown are estimations only. Always take real-life measurements of your wall before placing an order. Actual results may vary based on photo angle and reference accuracy.

How to Use the Visualizer
See your sign, logo, or graphic exactly how it will look — before you order.

What this tool is for: Interior office walls, reception areas, storefronts, retail spaces, and vehicle graphics. It can be used for any application where you need to visualise elements in a photo to scale — helpful for presentations or building an estimate based on available information. It is not intended for large building exteriors or architectural signage.

1

Upload a Photo

Take a straight-on photo of your wall, storefront, or vehicle at eye level. Drag and drop or click the upload zone. JPG or PNG works fine. Use ↺ New in the header anytime to clear everything and start fresh.

2

Set the Scale

Enter Wall Size — type the width & height in feet.
Click Reference — click two ends of any known object (door = 80", desk = 60") and enter its real length in inches.
Paper Sheet — tape a standard 8.5"×11" sheet to the surface, photograph it, then click both short edges.
Verify Scale — once scale is set, click two points on something else you know the size of (a door, a ceiling tile) and we'll tell you how close the scale is. Quote with confidence.

3

Add Your Design

Upload your logo (PNG with transparent background works best) or type text in any of 20 fonts. Drag to position. Resize from the gold corner handle. Delete with the red ✕. Click a text element to edit it inline.

4

Get the Size & Product Estimate

Select the sign type — Dimensional Letters/Logo, Cut Vinyl, Wall Mural, or Sign Element. The estimated size updates live as you drag and resize your overlay — useful for building a quote.

⟷

Point-to-Point Measurement

Once scale is set, click any two points on the photo to instantly measure the real-world distance between them — great for checking clearances and spacing.

⬚

Box Measure

Drag a rectangle over any area of the photo to measure its width and height. Save measurements with custom labels and toggle between inches and centimetres. Perfect for scoping available space before designing.

Zoom: Use +/− buttons or scroll wheel to zoom. Click ⬚ then drag a box to zoom into a specific area. Use ⌂ to reset the view.

Save your work: Use the save icon in the header to download a session file. Reload it anytime to pick up where you left off.

Best results: Shoot straight-on at eye level with even lighting. Avoid wide-angle or fisheye lenses.
